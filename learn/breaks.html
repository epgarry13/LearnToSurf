Breaks

The surf break is the zone - or lineup - where waves start to break. Take a look at the three main types of surf breaks and their characteristics.

A reef break is a surf spot where waves break over cobblestones, rocks, or coral seabeds. It is considered the most demanding break of all because they're extremely shallow and can cause unpredictable consequences. Not for beginners!

You may be familiar with this timeless classic - Point Break. Although an excellent flick, Patrick never actually explain what a point break is...

A point break is a surf spot where waves bend along a rocky headland or point or into curved shorelines like a natural bay and peel either to the right or left. It allows for long and racy rides, but are generally not suitable for first-timers.

So where can I surf????

At a beach break the waves break over sand - very forgiving for beginners. A famous example is Waikiki beach in Hawaii.
